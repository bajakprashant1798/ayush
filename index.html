Over the course of my ten years of problem solving, I've put together solutions in a variety of disciplines and experienced numerous failures, allowing me to see issues from a variety of perspectives.

But I wasn't always like this.

At the beginning

In my high school, we were taken to a styrofoam factory as part of factory visits. Knowing its environmental disadvantages, I was keen to develop a replacement. When my mother used to prepare 'naan,' I noticed that starch has binding qualities. This was my beginning point, and after 6 months, I created an eco-friendly alternative of styrofoam. Indeed…

Beginners’ luck

The project won a gold medal in the INSEF (Indian National Science and Engineering Fair) and was selected for the International Sustainable World Energy, Engineering, and Environment Project (I-SWEEEP), in which it won a silver medal. Back in India, I received a lot of attention, and that was all that mattered to me at the moment. however….

Realisation

In 2013, Kedarnath floods occurred, and seeing the aftermath, I decided to address issues with flash floods. I succeeded in discovering a solution using climate nowcasting and an autonomous weather system. I was even awarded at INSEF (Indian National Science and Engineering Fair) and at INESPO (International Environment and Sustainability Project Olympiad). But this time, I observed that…

Conflict of Interest

People congratulated me and went back to their work. There was no plan for execution. I was then advised to submit my study to journals in order to improve its odds of being implemented. This, however, needed a group effort. And, in order to form a team, I had to decide whether to work with engineers or designers. I sided with engineers because they supported the majority view. Unfortunately,...

Moving Forward

Contrary to my expectations, the setting turned out to be solitary. Nevertheless, I persuaded like-minded professors and classmates and, in 4 years, published 2 papers on "a decentralised water reuse system’ and "an ambulance assistance system," both were local derivations of high school projects, so as to aid their implementation. Here, even though...

Point of no Return

Each article was praised by a government agency in its field: Indian Water Works (IWWA) and Emergency Management and Research Institute (EMRI), but no action was taken. So, in a circumstance similar to four years ago, I had to choose between obtaining a job and implementing my solutions.This time, I proceeded with what I thought was necessary and decided to create a social-impact startup.

Hypothesis

We decided to work on malnutrition, a derivative of my research thesis, which I believed was most essential. We created a tasteless micronutrient premix by combining algorithms and culinary science. It was an effective solution for welfare homes because it did not require them to learn extra skills. In 3 years, we hoped to assist 1000 children/month with free fortified meals. Unfortunately…

Showdown

COVID came, our CSR funding collapsed; simultaneously, welfare homes were requesting our help. We could not sit idle and do nothing, so, learning from corporate strategies, we devised a self-sufficient plan that can work even without external aids. We had to iterate and pivot our plans three times, and eventually…

Happiness

We managed to develop a stable strategy in which we provided our premix to wholesalers such as Walart & Orion Pharma (India), Cibos Solutions (Canada), and Akshar Sp. Zoo (Europe). This in turn allowed us to feed nearly 200 children per month. We even…

Stagnation

Automated 80% of production, but yet we only increased our feeding strength to 250 children. As responsibilities eased, we had time to reflect. It turns out we were still far from our target of helping 1000 children/month. I also realised that going to design school would have better equipped me with necessary skills so as to grow my social impact startup more effectively…

IT IS NOT THE END

Fortunately, we learned a way to reach 10,000, if not 100,000, children per month. As the global south expands, governments will work with social consultancies to implement reforms. Creating one such company would thus give me an edge in enacting improved reforms. However, successfully building one such business would require improved negotiation, strategy, and networking skills. All of this has led me to pursue an MBA as the next stage in my goal of solving issues impacting the less fortunate.
